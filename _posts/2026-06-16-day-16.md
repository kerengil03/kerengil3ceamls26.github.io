  Today was our first day working in the research lab in person. We started the day by reviewing the tasks we needed to complete and discussing our roles within the project. Our focus for the day was on Augmentation and Class Weight Calculation, which are important processes used when preparing data for machine learning models. I learned that data augmentation involves creating slightly modified versions of training images to help improve a model's ability to recognize patterns and perform more accurately. Since our work was divided into steps, each team member was responsible for completing a specific task before the next person could continue. My responsibility was Step Four, where I applied the instructions from the previous steps and helped move the process forward. His explanations helped me better understand not only what we were doing, but also why each part of the process was important. Throughout the session, I gradually became more comfortable with the concepts and began understanding how augmentation can improve the quality and balance of a dataset. As we continued working, I was able to follow along more independently and connect the different parts of the activity together. Overall, today gave me a stronger understanding of how data preparation plays a major role in building effective AI and machine learning models.
