what_i_learned: |
  Today, I spent most of my time brainstorming ideas for our group's elevator pitch and thinking about the best way to present our research in a clear and engaging way. Since our project focuses on AI-based post-disaster building damage assessment, I wanted to make sure our pitch highlighted both the problem and the impact our research could have. I explored different ways to introduce the topic, explain why rapid damage assessment is important after natural disasters, and show how artificial intelligence can help improve response efforts. I also thought about how to make the presentation more engaging by using storytelling and visuals rather than simply presenting technical information. Throughout the day, I continued refining different ideas and considering what would best capture the audience's attention within a short amount of time. I also discussed ideas with my group members to make sure our elevator pitch clearly represented our research goals and contributions. Brainstorming multiple approaches helped me better understand how to communicate complex research in a simple and meaningful way. Overall, today's work strengthened both my creativity and my ability to communicate our project effectively.
  
  

blockers: |
  One challenge I experienced today was narrowing down the many ideas I had for our elevator pitch into one clear and concise message. I wanted to make sure the pitch was creative, engaging, and easy for a general audience to understand while still accurately representing our research. However, brainstorming different concepts and discussing them with my group helped me identify the strongest ideas to continue developing.

reflection: |
 Today showed me that presenting research is not just about explaining technical information but also about communicating its importance in a way that connects with an audience. Brainstorming ideas for our elevator pitch helped me think more creatively about our project and how to showcase its real-world impact. Overall, I feel more confident in helping develop a presentation that is both informative and engaging.
