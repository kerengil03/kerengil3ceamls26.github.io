what_i_learned: |
  Today, we started by introducing ourselves in person and getting to meet the members of our research groups, including our mentors. It was nice to finally put faces to names and begin building connections with the people I will be working with throughout the program. We also took individual headshots and group photos before heading inside to begin our activities for the day. Our first technical activity involved using Google Colab to learn about detecting breast cancer cells through AI and machine learning techniques. After our break, we participated in an interactive lesson about the limitations of ChatGPT and how AI systems can sometimes produce biased results, particularly for people of color and other underrepresented groups. I found this topic very interesting because it showed how important it is to evaluate AI systems critically and understand their limitations. We then worked in groups to test whether ChatGPT demonstrated bias when evaluating candidates for a Data Analyst position. During our activity, we found that my ChatGPT responses focused primarily on each applicant's experience and qualifications. Later, we completed the Marshmallow Challenge, where my team earned second place by creating a strong foundation and carefully building our structure upward. To end the day, we visited the engineering building to review expectations for the week and familiarize ourselves with the space where we will be working. Overall, today's activities helped me learn more about AI, teamwork, and the environment where our research will take place.
  
  

blockers: |
  At the moment, I do not have any major blockers. Some of the concepts related to AI bias and machine learning are still new to me, so I would like to continue learning more about how these systems work and how their limitations can impact different groups of people. However, I see this as an opportunity to expand my understanding rather than an obstacle.
  
reflection: |
 Today was a great introduction to the in-person portion of the program. I enjoyed meeting my group members and mentors, learning about AI applications such as breast cancer cell detection, and participating in discussions about the limitations and potential biases of AI systems. The Marshmallow Challenge also reminded me of the importance of teamwork, communication, and planning. Overall, I am excited to continue learning more about AI research and working alongside my group throughout the week.
