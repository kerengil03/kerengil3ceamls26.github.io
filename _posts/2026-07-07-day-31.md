what_i_learned: |
  Today, I spent most of my time revisiting my original ideas for our elevator pitch and starting over with a fresh perspective. I wanted to create a pitch that not only explained our research clearly but also captured the audience's attention from the very beginning. Throughout the day, I brainstormed several different concepts, each taking a unique approach to introducing our project. Some ideas focused on the urgency of disaster response, while others highlighted how artificial intelligence transforms satellite imagery into useful information for emergency responders. I also created a more technical version that explained our research pipeline, including how we use models such as U-Net, Siamese Networks, and Vision Transformers to classify building damage. As I developed each concept, I thought carefully about the flow of the presentation, the visuals that would accompany each scene, and the message we wanted the audience to take away. Creating multiple versions allowed me to compare different storytelling approaches and determine which one best represented our research. Overall, today's work helped me strengthen my creativity and better understand how to communicate a complex research project in a simple, engaging, and meaningful way.
  
  

blockers: |
  One challenge I experienced today was deciding which direction would be the most effective for our elevator pitch. I came up with several different ideas, and each one highlighted a different aspect of our research. It was sometimes difficult to choose between a more technical approach and one that focused more on storytelling and real-world impact. However, creating multiple versions helped me compare my ideas and determine which concepts would best communicate our project to a general audience.
  
reflection: |
 Today helped me realize that creating an effective elevator pitch requires both creativity and a clear understanding of the research. Brainstorming multiple concepts allowed me to think about different ways to explain our project while keeping the audience engaged. Overall, I feel more confident in developing a pitch that accurately represents our research and highlights the real-world impact of using AI for post-disaster building damage assessment.
