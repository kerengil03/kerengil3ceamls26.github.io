what_i_learned: |
  For today, we had gone into our groups, going over our schedule to make sure we are up to date with everything. First, we had started with reviewing who's doing what part of the literature review and what jobs and roles are assigned to which. Then, shortly after we went into our writing session where we learned a lot more about the iterative process, which is when you visualize your research to identify gaps. We also were assigned through the writing session an assignment to demonstrate the models and the work we have seen so far in our groups. We also have to, in the assignment, hypothesize a solution to the gaps remaining in our research and test it out. After this writing session, we went back into our research groups and straight into our week 2 research document and wrote a review on satellite-based building damage assessment. This was primarily about how updated resources such as CNN-based models have helped accelerate the process of analyzing pre-disaster and post-disaster images without having to handle everything manually. I also learned that these satellite images are analyzed based on texture, geographic region, and other image features. We ended the day by editing some of our progress so far in preparation for our Friday presentation.
  
  

blockers: |

Since I am still learning about CNN-based models and how pre-disaster and post-disaster images are analyzed, some of the information took extra time for me to fully understand. I also had to think critically about identifying research gaps and possible solutions during the writing session. However, these challenges helped me strengthen my understanding of both the research process and the technology being used.
